Solar Panel Cleaning

Why Solar Panel Cleaning Matters for Nebraska Homeowners in Kearney and Lincoln

April 2025 6 min read Kearney & Lincoln, NE

Nebraska gets more annual sunshine than most homeowners realize — making it genuinely excellent solar country. But here's what many solar panel owners in Kearney and Lincoln don't know: dirty panels can lose 15 to 25 percent of their energy output. For a system you paid tens of thousands of dollars to install, that's a significant reduction in the return on your investment every single month.

Solar panel cleaning in Kearney NE and Lincoln NE isn't a luxury — it's a straightforward maintenance step that pays for itself in recovered energy production. This guide explains why it matters, what specifically makes Nebraska challenging for solar panels, and how often you should be scheduling a professional cleaning.

How Dirt Affects Solar Panel Output

Solar panels generate electricity by converting sunlight into energy. When a layer of dust, pollen, bird droppings, or mineral deposits builds up on the panel's surface, that layer blocks a portion of the sunlight from reaching the photovoltaic cells underneath. Even a thin film of dust that's barely visible to the naked eye can reduce output by 10 to 15 percent.

What makes solar panel contamination particularly frustrating is that it's not evenly distributed. A streak of bird droppings across one section of a panel doesn't just reduce output from that section — depending on how the system is wired, a single dirty spot can pull down the output of an entire string of panels. This is called the "partial shading effect," and it means a few concentrated dirty spots can cause losses far greater than the area they cover.

Nebraska-Specific Problems for Solar Panels

Kearney and Lincoln present some specific challenges that make solar panel washing in Nebraska more important than in some other regions:

Agricultural Dust

Central Nebraska sits in one of the most productive agricultural regions in the country. Wind-carried field dust, crop residue, and soil particles coat solar panels throughout the growing season, especially in Kearney and surrounding communities where farmland is close to residential areas. This fine dust is particularly good at adhering to panel surfaces and is nearly invisible until it starts blocking sunlight.

Heavy Pollen Season

Nebraska's spring pollen season is intense. Cottonwood, elm, oak, and grass pollen create a thick film on outdoor surfaces — including solar panels — that rain often doesn't fully rinse away. Instead, rain causes pollen to form a sticky residue that bonds to the glass surface of panels.

Bird Activity

Bird droppings are one of the most damaging contaminants for solar panels because they're highly concentrated and acidic. Left on panels for extended periods, bird droppings can etch the anti-reflective coating that helps panels absorb maximum light. Solar panels on Nebraska homes — especially near tree cover — tend to accumulate bird droppings faster than homeowners expect.

Hard Water Deposits

Nebraska is well known for its hard water. When rain falls on panels and evaporates, it often leaves behind calcium and mineral deposits that cloud the glass. Over multiple seasons without cleaning, these deposits build up into a permanent-looking film that significantly reduces light transmission.

The math is simple: If your solar system produces $150/month in electricity savings and dirty panels reduce output by 20%, that's $30/month in lost savings — $360 per year. A professional solar panel cleaning in Kearney NE or Lincoln NE costs a fraction of that, and the efficiency recovery starts immediately after cleaning.

How Often Should You Clean Solar Panels in Nebraska?

For most Nebraska homeowners, solar panel cleaning once or twice per year is the right frequency. The most important cleaning windows are:

  • Late spring (May–June): After pollen season ends and cottonwood finishes. This removes the heaviest biological buildup of the year and sets your panels up for peak summer production during Nebraska's longest, sunniest days.
  • Early fall (September): Before the days get shorter. Cleaning off summer's accumulated dust and agricultural residue helps maximize production through the shoulder season.

If your panels are heavily shaded by trees, or if you're near a gravel road or active agricultural area, quarterly cleaning may be worthwhile. The best way to know: monitor your inverter's output data and look for unexplained production dips during sunny periods — that's often a sign of soiling losses.

DIY vs. Professional Solar Panel Cleaning in Nebraska

Some homeowners try to clean their solar panels themselves, but there are real risks with DIY solar panel washing:

  • Roof access: Most residential solar arrays are on steep roof sections. Climbing onto a wet or sloped roof to clean panels is dangerous without proper equipment.
  • Wrong cleaning products: Standard household cleaners, hard tap water, and abrasive scrubbers can all damage anti-reflective coatings and cause permanent scratching. Panels must be cleaned with purified (deionized) water and soft-bristle tools.
  • Warranty concerns: Some solar panel warranties require professional cleaning or specify cleaning methods. Using the wrong technique can void coverage.
